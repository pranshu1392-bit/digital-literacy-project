UPI Fraud Case Study


The most common cybercrime in India involving the UPI (Unified Payments Interface) is UPI fraud, which usually occurs when attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as OTPs, PINs, etc.

The process of fraud typically begins when an attacker contacts the user, pretending to be a bank representative or customer support executive. They may ask the user to click on a link or approve a payment request.

In some cases, the attacker sends a “collect request” on a UPI app, claiming that the user needs to accept it to receive money.

The user, unaware of the scam, enters their UPI PIN, which actually authorizes a payment instead of receiving money.

Students and elderly people are often targeted, as they may not fully understand how digital payment systems work.

The impact of such cybercrimes includes financial loss and difficulty in recovering the lost money.
